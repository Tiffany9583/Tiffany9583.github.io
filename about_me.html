About me

關於乙庭

February 4, 2023 Yiting

I am Yi-Ting, a current third-year PhD student in Entomology at National Chung Hsing University (NCHU), Taiwan. In my PhD, I research on the effect of beneficial symbiont on developing a cost-effective rearing system of Orius strigicollis. I designed selective culture media to isolate specific symbionts based on metagenomic data of O. strigicollis gut microbiota. To understand the function of the symbiont in the host immune system and nutritional supplementation, I investigated differential gene expression of O. strigicollis in several manipulative experiments (e.g. symbiont removal and re-adding trials). Although that work is not yet published, the goal we have already achieved.
“Outstanding performance in a single field does not guarantee that you will succeed in the future.” When I just began the PhD, I realized programming is so powerful and necessary in current scientific research. At that time,I selected the program of intelligent innovation to be a minor in my PhD. The courses of computer programming developed my programming skills and inspired me to notice the value of the application of artificial Intelligence in other fields. Since then, I have been particularly interested in how and when we could integrate artificial Intelligence with insect pest control and ecology.
