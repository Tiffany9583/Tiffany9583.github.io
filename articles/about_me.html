About me

關於乙庭

December 27, 2025 Yi-Ting

I am Yi-Ting Hung, a current postdoctoral fellow in Center for Neuropsychiatric Research at National Health Research Institutes (NHRI), Taiwan. My academic journey began with a Bachelor’s degree in Plant Medicine, where I specialized in plant pathology and entomology. I continued my graduate studies in Entomology, where I developed a strong interdisciplinary interest in integrating entomological research with computational sciences.

Throughout my academic training, I have engaged in diverse research projects spanning ecological modeling, agricultural pest control using natural enemies, metagenomic analysis, primer design for E. coli cloning, and the application of deep learning models to insect behavior analysis, particularly in Drosophila melanogaster. These experiences have equipped me with a broad and versatile skill set in both computational biology and experimental entomology.

I have always aspired to be an interdisciplinary scientist. I believe that researchers who are fluent in both wet-lab experimentation and computational methods are uniquely positioned to solve complex biological problems, especially in an era where AI is reshaping the scientific landscape. My academic trajectory has reinforced my passion for integrating biology with data science.
